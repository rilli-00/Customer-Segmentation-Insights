## Project Overview

This project applies machine learning techniques to analyze customer purchasing behavior and identify distinct customer segments. By grouping customers with similar demographic characteristics, income levels, and shopping patterns, businesses can better understand their customer base and develop targeted marketing strategies.

The dataset contains demographic and purchasing information for more than 2,200 customers across multiple product categories and sales channels.

---

## Business Objective

The objective of this project is to transform raw customer data into actionable business insights. By identifying meaningful customer segments, organizations can:

* Improve customer targeting.
* Personalize marketing campaigns.
* Increase customer retention.
* Optimize promotional strategies.
* Support data-driven business decision-making.

---

## Customer Segments

The clustering process identified four main customer groups:

### Premium VIP Customers

High-income customers with strong purchasing power and consistently high spending across multiple product categories.

### Value-Seeking Families

Established families that actively respond to promotions, discounts, and special offers when making purchasing decisions.

### Large Families

Customers managing larger households with spending patterns focused on essential goods and bulk purchases.

### Young Budget Families

Younger families with limited budgets who carefully compare prices and focus on cost-effective purchasing behavior.

## Methodology

The project follows a complete machine learning workflow:

1. Data Cleaning and Preprocessing
2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
3. Feature Engineering
4. Feature Scaling using StandardScaler
5. Dimensionality Reduction using PCA
6. Customer Segmentation using Agglomerative Clustering
7. Cluster Profiling and Interpretation
8. Business Insight Generation
